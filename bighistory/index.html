Big History

From the first spark of energy to the complexity of the global age.

Threshold 1 13.8 Billion Years Ago

The Big Bang

The universe appears from nothing.

Matter, energy, space, and time are created in an instant. The universe is a hot, dense soup of particles. As it cools, the first atoms (Hydrogen and Helium) begin to form, setting the stage for everything that follows.
Threshold 2 13.6 Billion Years Ago

The First Stars

Gravity collapses clouds of gas.

Gravity pulls hydrogen atoms together until they fuse, releasing massive amounts of energy. These first stars provide the "hotspots" in a cold universe, allowing for the creation of more complex structures.
Threshold 3 10 Billion Years Ago

New Chemical Elements

Dying stars create the periodic table.

When large stars die, they explode as supernovae. These explosions create high enough temperatures to forge complex elements like iron, gold, and carbon, which are necessary for planets and life.
Threshold 4 4.5 Billion Years Ago

The Solar System

A star is born from space debris.

Our Sun forms from a cloud of gas and dust. The remaining 0.1% of matter circles the sun, colliding and accreting into the rocky and gaseous planets of our solar system, including Earth.
Threshold 5 3.8 Billion Years Ago

Life Emerges

Organic molecules start to replicate.

Deep-sea vents or shallow pools provide the perfect conditions for chemistry to become biology. Life begins as single-celled organisms, later evolving photosynthesis, which oxygenates our atmosphere.
Evolution 65 Million Years Ago

The Dinosaurs Perish

An asteroid clears the way for mammals.

A massive impact in the Yucatan Peninsula causes a global extinction event. While tragic for dinosaurs, it allows small, burrowing mammals to thrive and eventually evolve into primates.
Threshold 6 200,000 Years Ago

Collective Learning

Homo sapiens develop language.

Humans gain the ability to share complex ideas through symbolic language. This means information is no longer lost when an individual dies; it accumulates over generations, accelerating our control over the environment.
Threshold 7 10,000 Years Ago

The Agricultural Revolution

Farming replaces foraging.

As the last Ice Age ends, humans begin domesticating plants and animals. This leads to food surpluses, permanent settlements, and the rise of cities, states, and writing.
Threshold 8 250 Years Ago

The Anthropocene

The Modern Revolution.

Humans tap into the energy stored in fossil fuels. This leads to the Industrial Revolution, a massive population explosion, and global connectivity, but also places immense strain on the Earth's biosphere.
Current Era Today

The Global Age

A species connected by the web.

We are now a globally interconnected species. We have the power to explore the stars or destroy our own habitat. The future of the "ninth threshold" depends on how we manage our collective knowledge today.
