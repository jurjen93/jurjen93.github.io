About

Welcome! I am Jurjen. I work as a radio astronomer and software developer at Leiden Observatory and ASTRON, where I work on developing and improving data-reduction strategies and automated techniques for processing radio interferometric data. I am currently focusing on improving pipelines for the European LOw Frequency ARray (LOFAR) to produce wide-field high-resolution images on HPC clusters. My research also explores the cosmic evolution of radio galaxies and energetic phenomena like galaxy cluster mergers.

Beyond astronomy, I am a co-founder of Stamily, a platform created for and by people who stutter, supporting connection and community for people who stutter around the world. I also enjoy travelling. You can find more about that here.
